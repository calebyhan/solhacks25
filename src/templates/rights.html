Know Your Rights

It's important to know your rights in case you come across a situation where you encounter an ICE officer. Many people are unaware of the legal protections that are provided to them, and to avoid unlawful deportation and punishment, it's crucial to educate yourself on your rights.

1. Right to stay silent.

You do not have to answer any questions about your immigration status or birth place.

2. Right to refuse a search

The ICE do not have the right to search you, your home, or your car without a warrant signed by a judge.

3. Right to an attorney and a court hearing

You have the right to speak to a lawyer before answering any questions or signing documents. You also have the right to fight your case in court and should not agree to a voluntary deportation without discussing with a lawyer.

4. Right to refuse to show documents

If you do not have your documents on you, you have the right to refuse show documents. Do NOT provide fake documents or lie. This can be used against you.
